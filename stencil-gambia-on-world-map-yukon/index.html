Gambia On World Map: A Travel Guide

Posted on February 23, 2023

Introduction

The Gambia is a small country located in West Africa. It is surrounded by Senegal on all sides except for its short coastline on the Atlantic Ocean. Despite its small size, Gambia has a lot to offer for travellers who are looking for a unique and authentic African experience.

Where is Gambia?

Gambia is located on the west coast of Africa, south of Senegal and north of Guinea-Bissau. It is a small country, covering only 11,295 square kilometers. Gambia has a population of approximately 2 million people and its capital city is Banjul.

Why Visit Gambia?

Gambia is a great destination for travellers who are looking for a tropical getaway without the crowds of some of the more popular destinations. The country has a rich cultural heritage, beautiful beaches, and a variety of wildlife. In addition, Gambia is known for its friendly people, making it a great place to meet locals and learn about their way of life.

Getting to Gambia

By Air

The easiest way to get to Gambia is by flying into Banjul International Airport. There are several airlines that offer flights to Banjul, including British Airways, Air France, and Brussels Airlines. Once you arrive in Banjul, you can take a taxi or public transportation to your hotel.

By Land

If you are travelling from Senegal, you can enter Gambia by road. There are several border crossings between the two countries, including the one at Amdalai. You can take a shared taxi or bus from Dakar to the border and then cross on foot. Once you are in Gambia, you can take a bus or taxi to your destination.

Things to Do in Gambia

Visit the Beaches

Gambia is known for its beautiful beaches. Some of the most popular ones include Kololi Beach, Kotu Beach, and Sanyang Beach. These beaches offer a variety of activities, including swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Explore the National Parks

Gambia has several national parks where you can see a variety of wildlife, including monkeys, baboons, and birds. Some of the most popular parks include Kiang West National Park, Abuko Nature Reserve, and River Gambia National Park.

Experience the Local Culture

Gambia has a rich cultural heritage, and there are several ways to experience it. You can visit local markets, attend a traditional dance performance, or take a cooking class to learn how to make local dishes.

Where to Stay in Gambia

Hotels

There are several hotels in Gambia, ranging from budget to luxury. Some of the most popular ones include the Kairaba Beach Hotel, Coco Ocean Resort & Spa, and Ocean Bay Hotel & Resort.

Guesthouses

If you are looking for a more authentic experience, you can stay in a guesthouse. There are several guesthouses in Gambia, including the Lemon Creek Hotel Resort and Keur Saloum.

FAQs

Is Gambia Safe to Visit?

Yes, Gambia is generally a safe country to visit. However, as with any destination, it is important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

What Language is Spoken in Gambia?

The official language of Gambia is English, but many Gambians also speak local languages such as Mandinka and Wolof.

What Currency is Used in Gambia?

The currency of Gambia is the Gambian dalasi (GMD). US dollars and Euros are also widely accepted. It is recommended that you exchange money at a bank or exchange bureau to get the best rates.

What is the Best Time to Visit Gambia?

The best time to visit Gambia is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. The temperatures are cooler during this time, and there is less rainfall. The wet season, from June to October, can be hot and humid, with heavy rainfalls.
