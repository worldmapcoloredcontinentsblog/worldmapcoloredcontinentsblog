The Mississippi River

The Mississippi River is one of the longest rivers in the USA, stretching over 2,300 miles from Minnesota to the Gulf of Mexico. It is a vital transportation route for goods and people, and its waters support a vast array of wildlife. The river is also home to many cultural and historical sites, including the Gateway Arch in St. Louis and the French Quarter in New Orleans.
