Introduction

What are Wild Horses?

Wild horses are free-roaming horses that live in the wild, without any human intervention, and are not considered as domesticated animals. These horses are descendants of the horses that were brought to America by the Spanish in the 16th century.

Where can you Find Wild Horses in Arizona?

Arizona is home to several herds of wild horses, and they can be found in various locations across the state. Some of the popular locations where you can spot these horses are Tonto National Forest, Apache-Sitgreaves National Forest, and Salt River.

What is the Best Time to Spot Wild Horses in Arizona?

The best time to spot wild horses in Arizona is during the early morning or late afternoon, as these are the times when the horses come out to graze and drink water. It is also recommended to visit during the cooler months of the year, as the horses are more active during this time.

What Precautions should you take while Spotting Wild Horses?

While spotting wild horses in Arizona, it is important to maintain a safe distance from them and avoid feeding them. It is also recommended to stay quiet and avoid making any sudden movements that could startle them.
