Bali On World Map: A Tourist Haven

Posted on October 28, 2022

Introduction

Bali, a province of Indonesia, is a small island that lies between Java and Lombok Island. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations globally, known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and traditional art forms. Bali is located on the world map at 8.3405° S, 115.0920° E.

Geographical Location

Bali is part of the Lesser Sunda Islands, which lie between Java and Australia. The island is located in the westernmost end of the Lesser Sunda Islands, and its nearest neighbor is Java, which lies to the west. Bali is located about 1,600 km east of Jakarta, the capital city of Indonesia.

Size and Topography

Bali is a small island, covering an area of 5,780 km². The island’s topography is diverse, with a central mountain range that runs from east to west, dividing the island into two regions. The northern part of the island is relatively flat, and the southern part is hilly and mountainous, with the highest peak, Mount Agung, reaching 3,142 meters above sea level.

Climate and Weather

Bali has a tropical climate, with two distinct seasons, the wet season and the dry season. The wet season usually lasts from October to April, and the dry season from May to September. The average temperature in Bali is around 27°C, and the humidity is high throughout the year.

Flora and Fauna

Bali’s natural beauty is a result of its diverse flora and fauna. The island is home to several species of animals, including the Bali Starling, Bali Myna, and the Bali Tiger. The island’s vegetation is equally diverse, with lush rainforests, rice paddies, and beautiful beaches.

Culture and Tradition

Bali is renowned for its unique culture and tradition, which is a blend of Hinduism and Balinese customs. The island is home to numerous temples, which are an integral part of the Balinese culture. The Balinese people are also known for their traditional dance forms, such as the Legong, Kecak, and Barong dance.

Tourism Industry

Bali’s tourism industry is one of the most robust in the world, contributing significantly to Indonesia’s economy. The island attracts millions of tourists annually, who come to experience its natural beauty, culture, and tradition. Bali offers a range of activities for tourists, including surfing, scuba diving, and hiking.

COVID-19 Impact

The COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on Bali’s tourism industry. The island had to close its borders to foreign tourists, resulting in a significant drop in tourism revenue. However, the island has taken measures to revive its tourism industry, such as implementing health protocols and promoting domestic tourism.

Conclusion

Bali’s position on the world map has made it a tourist haven, attracting millions of visitors annually. The island’s natural beauty, culture, and tradition, combined with its tourism industry, make it a popular destination for travelers worldwide.
