The Fascinating Middle Ages European Map

Posted on September 20, 2022

Introduction

The Middle Ages was a period in European history that lasted from the 5th to the 15th century. It was characterized by feudalism, the rise of Christianity, and the emergence of powerful monarchies. During this time, Europe underwent significant changes, and its maps reflected these changes. In this article, we will explore the Middle Ages European map and its features.

The European Map During the Early Middle Ages

At the beginning of the Middle Ages, the European map was limited to the Roman Empire’s borders. However, as the empire declined, new kingdoms emerged, and the map expanded. The early medieval map of Europe was dominated by the Germanic tribes who had migrated into the region. These tribes established powerful kingdoms such as the Franks, the Visigoths, and the Ostrogoths.

The Rise of Christianity and Its Impact on the Map

Christianity played a significant role in the Middle Ages. As the religion spread throughout Europe, it had a profound impact on the map. Monasteries were established, and they became centers of learning and knowledge. Monks created maps that reflected their understanding of the world, and these maps were used for navigation purposes. The maps featured religious symbols and were often oriented towards the east, where Jerusalem, the Holy City, was located.

The Crusades and Their Influence on the Map

The Crusades were a series of religious wars fought between the Christian and Muslim armies in the 11th to the 13th centuries. These wars had a significant impact on the Middle Ages European map. The Crusaders traveled to the Holy Land, and as they did, they created maps that showed the routes they took. These maps were often inaccurate and included mythical creatures such as unicorns and sea monsters.

The Emergence of Powerful Monarchies and Their Influence on the Map

During the Middle Ages, powerful monarchies emerged in Europe. These monarchies had a significant impact on the map. They commissioned maps that reflected their power and influence. The maps were often decorated with the monarch’s coat of arms and showed their territories. These maps were used for political purposes, and they helped the monarchs maintain their power.

The Renaissance and the Changing Map

The Renaissance was a period of rebirth that occurred in Europe between the 14th and 17th centuries. During this time, there was a renewed interest in classical learning, and this had a profound impact on the European map. The maps created during the Renaissance were more accurate and realistic. They included details such as longitude and latitude, and they were oriented towards the north, where the magnetic compass pointed.

Conclusion

The Middle Ages European map was a reflection of the changes that occurred in Europe during this period. It was influenced by religion, politics, and the rise of powerful monarchies. The maps created during this time were inaccurate and often included mythical creatures, but they were an important step towards creating the more accurate maps of the Renaissance. Today, these maps serve as a reminder of Europe’s rich history and the changes that occurred during the Middle Ages.
