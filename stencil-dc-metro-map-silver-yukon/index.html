Introduction

What is the Silver Line?

The Silver Line is a rapid transit line that runs through the Washington D.C. metropolitan area. It connects riders from downtown D.C. to locations in Virginia, including Tysons Corner, Reston, and Dulles Airport. The Silver Line is a relatively new addition to the D.C. Metro system, having opened in 2014.

How to Use the Silver Line

Using the Silver Line is easy. Simply locate a Silver Line station on the D.C. Metro Map and make your way to the platform. You can purchase a farecard or use a SmarTrip card to pay for your ride. Once you’re on the train, sit back and relax as you travel to your destination.

Benefits of the Silver Line

The Silver Line offers numerous benefits to riders. One of the most significant advantages is the ability to avoid traffic congestion on the roads. Additionally, the Silver Line provides easy access to popular destinations, including Dulles Airport and Tysons Corner. Finally, the Silver Line is an affordable option for those traveling to and from the city.

FAQs

What are the hours of operation for the Silver Line?

The Silver Line operates from 5:00 am to 11:30 pm on weekdays, 7:00 am to 11:30 pm on Saturdays, and 8:00 am to 11:00 pm on Sundays.

How much does it cost to ride the Silver Line?

The cost of riding the Silver Line varies depending on the distance traveled. The minimum fare is $2.25, and the maximum fare is $6.00.

Is the Silver Line accessible to those with disabilities?

Yes, all Silver Line stations are accessible to those with disabilities. Elevators and escalators are available at all stations to make it easy for everyone to travel.

How often do Silver Line trains run?

Silver Line trains run every 6-12 minutes during peak hours and every 12-15 minutes during off-peak hours.

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your Silver Line experience:
